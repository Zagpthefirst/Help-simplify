Nomenclature of organic compounds:

Organic compound:
Compound containing at least 1 C-H bond
IUPAC:
Organization outlying chemistry and its use
Aliphatic
Compounds which do not have continuos conjugation around a ring
Alicyclic:
Aliphatic compounds that have 1 ring
Bicylic:
Aliphatic compounds containing 2 rings and 2 carbons common to both rings
Tricyclic:
Aliphatic compounds containing 3 rings and 2 carbons common to the rings
Spiro:
Aliphatic compounds containing 2 or more rings and having 1 carbon common between rings

Purification of organic compounds and Identification of unkown compounds

Extraction:
Removal of desired compound from a mixture
Alkaloids:
Naturally occurring organic compounds with at least 1 nitrogen in them
Solvent:
Fluid in which things are dissolved
Purification:
Increasing concentration of desired compound
Sublimation:
Direct conversion of phase of substance from solid phase to gas phase
Crystallisation:
Formation of crystals of a substance
Distillation:
Boiling a mixture of substances with very different boiling points in a special setup in order to extract them
Fractional distillation:
Distillation performed with a fractionating column added to the setup for extraction of substances with similar boiling points from a mixture
Steam volatile:
Compound which is easily vapourised on passing steam through it
Steam distillation:
Distillation in which steam is passed through steam volatile compound and compound is extracted from steam by condensing the steam and using a separating funnel
Stationary Phase
A substance that does not move during chromatography.
Moving phase
Fluid that is passed over the stationary phase during chromatography
Eluant
Solvent used to remove an adsorbed substance
Chromatography
Method of extraction based on polarity difference.Compound is placed onto the stationary phase and the moving phase is passed over it
Gas chromatography
Chromatography where the mixture of gases and the moving phase passes through a long column coated by the stationary phase and a detector detects the time when gases exit along with their concentrations and records them onto a chart recorder
Molecular weight
Weight of the molecule in grams/mol
Volatile
Easily forms fumes
